About Me

Hi, I’m Jacqueline Sapon Rodriguez, a 17-year-old Hispanic student with a passion for computer science and technology.

Originally, I dreamed of becoming an architect, but after attending a summer program at Rice University, I discovered that computer science is where my curiosity and creativity truly align. Since then, I have been actively exploring coding, robotics, and technology to build solutions that can help others.

I have taken AP Computer Science Principles and AP Computer Science A, and I am proud to be Java certified and Fusion 360 certified. I also participated in NASA’s 6-Month High School Aerospace Scholars Program, where I deepened my interest in engineering and space technology.

To further explore my interests, I attended the UT Computer Science for Women Program, as well as the Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program and One-Month Self-Paced Program. During my freshman year, I began strengthening my skills by enrolling in online summer coding camps, including Creative Coding with JavaScript and Java Basics, which laid the foundation for my programming journey.

My goal is to create tools, projects, and resources that make people’s lives easier while inspiring other young women, especially those from underrepresented backgrounds, to explore the world of technology.

Outside of coding, I enjoy helping my siblings with their studies, learning new design tools, and working on personal projects that blend technology and creativity.

Thank you for taking the time to learn a little about me. Feel free to check out my projects and certifications, and reach out if you would like to connect!
